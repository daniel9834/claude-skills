# Instagram

## How the Algorithm Works

Instagram runs separate ranking systems for Feed, Reels, Stories, and Explore — each weighing signals differently. Content enters a small test audience first, then expands based on performance.

**What matters most:**

1. **Shares to DMs** — "Sends per reach" is now one of the strongest discovery signals. Content people share privately gets pushed harder than content people like publicly.
2. **Saves** — Still a top-tier signal for Feed and Explore. Save-worthy content = algorithmic gold.
3. **Completion rate** — For Reels, how much of the video people watch. For carousels, how many slides they swipe through.
4. **Engagement quality** — Likes-per-reach matters more than raw like count. A post with 50 likes from 500 impressions beats 200 likes from 50,000 impressions.
5. **Relationship history** — DMs, profile visits, comment history with an account increase your visibility to those specific users.
6. **First 30-90 minutes** — Early velocity determines whether the post gets pushed to a wider audience.

**Current landscape:** Photos are getting renewed support in Feed (confirmed by Instagram leadership). Carousels expanded to 20 slides in some regions. Hashtag influence has dropped significantly — content quality signals matter more than tags. A new "Your Algorithm" feature lets users see why they're seeing content.

## Formats

| Format | Dimensions | Sweet Spot |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| Carousel | 1080x1350px (4:5), 6-10 slides (up to 20) | Highest engagement and saves |
| Reel | 1080x1920px (9:16), 7-15 sec or 30-45 sec | Best for discovery and new followers |
| Single image | 1080x1350px | Getting more support in current algorithm |
| Story | 1080x1920px, under 15 sec per frame | Best for DM engagement and polls |

## Carousel Structure

| Slide | Job |
|-------|-----|
| 1 (Cover) | Stop the scroll — bold text, one idea, high contrast, minimal design |
| 2 | Deepen the hook — "here's the problem" or "what most people miss" |
| 3-8 | One point per slide — large readable text, visual hierarchy, minimal words |
| 9 | Recap — all points summarized for quick scanning |
| 10 | CTA — "Save this," "Share with someone who needs it," "Follow @handle" |

**Design specifications:**
- **Dimensions:** 1080x1350px (4:5 ratio) — maximum feed space on mobile. All slides same ratio
- **Cover slide:** This IS the grid thumbnail. It must work at small size. Bold text, one idea, high contrast. No fine detail that disappears when shrunk
- **Headlines:** 40-60pt bold. One line preferred, two max. Must be readable without tapping to expand
- **Body text:** 24-32pt. Short phrases, not paragraphs. If you can't read it at arm's length on a phone, it's too small
- **Colors:** Use brand palette from style guide if available. If not, stick to high-contrast combinations. Avoid pastels on white — they wash out on phone screens. Dark backgrounds with white or bright text often outperform
- **Fonts:** Sans-serif for impact. One font for headlines, one for body, no more. Instagram carousels should feel designed, not like a document
- **Whitespace:** More than you think you need. Cramped slides feel cheap. Let each point breathe
- **Visual elements:** Icons, simple illustrations, or highlight boxes can break up text-heavy slides. But keep it simple — no stock photos, no busy backgrounds
- **Consistency:** Same layout grid, same color scheme, same fonts across every slide. The set should feel cohesive when swiped through
- **Reel thumbnails:** If posting Reels, the cover frame matters for the grid. Use a custom cover image (1080x1920px) with bold text — don't let Instagram auto-select a random frame

## Caption Structure

```
[HOOK — must work in the 2-line preview before "...more"]

.
.
.

[BODY — the value, story, or insight]

[2-4 short paragraphs, each 2-3 sentences max]

---

Save this for later
Share with a friend who needs it
Drop a comment if this hit home

---

#[niche] #[broader] #[topic] (3-5 hashtags, end of caption)
```

The first line of the caption is everything. Most people see only the preview — if the hook doesn't earn the tap, the rest doesn't matter.

## Reel Script (15-30 seconds)

```
SECONDS 0-3 — HOOK
"[Pattern interrupt or bold claim that earns attention]"
[Visual: movement, text overlay, or unexpected opening frame]

SECONDS 3-20 — SUBSTANCE
"Here's what I mean:
First — [point, delivered fast]
Second — [point, keep momentum]
Third — [the payoff]"

SECONDS 20-30 — CLOSE
"Follow for more [topic]"
or "Save this"
or "Send to someone who needs this"
```

**The 3-second rule:** 65% of viewers decide to keep watching or leave in the first 3 seconds. Front-load both the visual and verbal hook. Start mid-action, not with an intro.

## Story Sequence

```
Story 1 — Hook with poll or question sticker
"Quick question..." + [Poll: Option A / Option B]

Story 2 — Setup
"Here's why I ask..." + [brief context]

Stories 3-5 — Value
One point per story, use text animation or stickers

Story 6 — CTA
"Want the full breakdown?" + [Link sticker]
or "DM me [keyword] for [resource]"
```

Stories drive DM engagement, which strengthens your relationship signal with followers. Use interactive stickers (polls, questions, sliders) to boost this.

## Opening Patterns

**1. DM Magnet**
> "[Bold claim]. DM me '[keyword]' and I'll send you the full breakdown."

**2. Save Trigger**
> "Bookmark this — you'll need it when [specific future scenario]."

**3. Visual Contradiction**
> [Image/text that says one thing] + caption that reveals why it's wrong

**4. Identity Callout**
> "This is for everyone who [specific relatable frustration]. You're not crazy — here's what's actually happening."

**5. Transformation Tease**
> "I went from [before state] to [after state] by changing one thing. Swipe to see what."

**6. Unpacked Stat**
> "[Surprising number or data point]. Let me explain what that actually means for you."

**7. Before/After Visual**
> [Show the transformation in the first slide or first frame. Then explain the process]

**8. Myth Buster**
> "Everyone says [common advice]. Here's why that's actually hurting you."

**9. Series Tease**
> "Part 1 of [N]: [Topic]. This series is going to change how you think about [subject]."

**10. Direct Value Promise**
> "Save this carousel — it's the only guide you need for [specific outcome]."

## What Not to Do on Instagram

- **Don't lead with text walls.** Instagram is visual-first. Even carousels need strong design — a plain text slide with no visual hierarchy gets swiped past.
- **Don't rely on hashtags for reach.** They're nearly irrelevant now. Focus on making content people share via DM — that's the real distribution mechanism.
- **Don't post Reels just to post Reels.** A weak Reel hurts your account's engagement rate. If the content doesn't translate to video naturally, make a carousel instead.
- **Don't cross-post from LinkedIn.** The formatting, tone, and expectations are completely different. Rebuild the content for Instagram's visual culture.
- **Don't bury the hook.** The caption preview shows ~2 lines. If the hook is in line 4, nobody sees it.
- **Don't skip the CTA.** "Save this" and "Share with a friend" are the two most valuable actions on Instagram. Ask for them explicitly.
