# Threads

## How the Algorithm Works

Threads uses a mix of Instagram's social graph and its own engagement signals to rank content. Your Instagram followers see your Threads posts by default, giving cross-platform distribution built in.

**What matters most:**

1. **Reply depth** — Multi-reply conversations signal high-quality content. Posts that spark genuine back-and-forth get pushed harder.
2. **Reshares** — The primary amplification mechanism. When someone reshares your post, it reaches their audience.
3. **Quote-posts** — Adding your commentary when resharing boosts reach significantly more than a plain reshare.
4. **Cross-graph reach** — Engagement from people *outside* your Instagram follower graph signals broader appeal and unlocks discovery distribution.
5. **Topic relevance** — Posts matching trending topics surface in the discovery feed.
6. **Instagram relationship signals** — DMs, follows, and interactions on Instagram carry over to Threads ranking.

**Current landscape:** Lower competition than X. Instagram cross-posting drives initial distribution. Text-first format rewards thoughtful, opinion-led content. The culture is more conversational and constructive than X — genuine discussion outperforms broadcasting. No DMs (engagement is public only). No ads yet — organic reach is high. Fediverse integration (ActivityPub) means posts are visible on Mastodon.

## Formats

| Format | Specs | When to use |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| Single post | 200-400 characters, under 500 limit | Quick takes, observations, questions |
| Mini-thread | 3-5 connected posts | When one post isn't enough for the idea |
| Quote-post | Your take + reshare of another post | High amplification — adds value to existing conversation |
| Image post | 1080x1350px, up to 10 images | Breaks up the text-heavy feed |
| Video post | Up to 5 minutes, 1080x1920px | Less common but stands out in a text-heavy feed |

**Visual specs:**
- **Image dimensions:** 1080x1350px (4:5) for maximum feed presence, or 1080x1080px (1:1). Both work
- **Text on images:** Large, clean, minimal. Threads is text-first — if you're adding an image, it should genuinely add something the text can't. A screenshot, a chart, a visual framework. Not a generic stock photo with a quote on it
- **Multi-image posts:** Up to 10 images. Good for visual breakdowns, step-by-step processes, or data comparisons. Each image should be self-explanatory
- **Quote graphics:** If sharing a quote as an image, keep the design dead simple. White background, black text, small attribution. Overdesigned quote graphics feel out of place on Threads

## Post Templates

**Conversational Take**
```
[Opinion or observation in plain, casual language]

I've been thinking about this because [context].

The part nobody mentions: [deeper insight].

What's your experience?
```

**Mini-Thread**
```
Post 1: [Bold observation or claim]

Post 2: Here's what I mean —
[Supporting point with a concrete example]

Post 3: And the thing that surprises me:
[Deeper or unexpected angle]

Post 4: Bottom line: [clear takeaway]
```

**Quote-Post Commentary**
```
[Reshare someone else's post]

This is underrated. Here's why:
[Your perspective in 2-3 sentences that adds
something the original post didn't say]
```

**Genuine Question**
```
Honest question for [group]:

[Specific, non-rhetorical question]

I've been [context for why you're curious].
Genuinely want to hear what others think.
```

## Opening Patterns

**1. Thinking Out Loud**
> "Something I can't stop thinking about regarding [topic]:"

**2. Respectful Disagreement**
> "I really respect [person/company] but I think they're off on [specific thing]. Here's my read:"

**3. Behind the Curtain**
> "OK honest question — does anyone else [relatable experience]?"

**4. Cross-Platform Bridge**
> "I wrote about this on Instagram but wanted to go deeper here..."

**5. Warm Observation**
> "One pattern I keep noticing in [field/community]:"

**6. Invitation to Disagree**
> "I might be wrong about this, but [take]. Change my mind?"

**7. Simple Reframe**
> "What if [common thing] isn't about [assumed purpose] at all? What if it's actually about [different angle]?"

**8. Experience Contrast**
> "What I expected about [topic] vs what actually happened:"

**9. Community Shoutout**
> "Shoutout to everyone in [niche] who [specific shared experience]. Here's something I learned that might help:"

**10. Quick Win**
> "One small change I made to [process] that made a surprising difference:"

## What Not to Do on Threads

- **Don't broadcast.** Threads rewards conversation, not announcements. "Here are 5 tips" performs worse than "I noticed something interesting about [topic] — what do you think?"
- **Don't use X's tone.** The platform cultures are genuinely different. Hot takes and combative energy that work on X often fall flat on Threads. The audience expects warmer, more thoughtful interaction.
- **Don't ignore replies.** The algorithm prioritizes reply depth. If people reply and you don't engage, you're leaving algorithmic value on the table.
- **Don't treat it as an Instagram caption channel.** Cross-posting your carousel caption as a Threads post looks lazy. Threads is text-first — write for the platform.
- **Don't use hashtags.** Threads has a limited topic tag system, not hashtags. Tags are optional and function differently than Instagram or X hashtags.
- **Don't expect immediate scale.** Threads is still growing. The upside is lower competition and higher per-post engagement. Think long-term audience building.
