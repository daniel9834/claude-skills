# YouTube

## How the Algorithm Works

YouTube's recommendation system optimizes for viewer satisfaction — a combination of click-through rate, watch duration, and post-watch behavior (did they watch another video? did they leave the platform?).

**What matters most:**

1. **Click-through rate (CTR)** — How often people click when shown your thumbnail and title. This determines initial distribution. Target: 4-10% from Home feed, 6-12% is strong.
2. **Watch duration** — Both raw minutes watched and percentage completed. A video watched 80% is stronger than one watched 30%, even if the 30% video is longer.
3. **Session impact** — Does your video keep people on YouTube? Videos that lead to more watching get recommended more.
4. **Viewer satisfaction** — YouTube uses surveys and behavioral signals (likes, shares, "not interested" clicks, skip speed) to estimate quality beyond raw metrics.
5. **Topical authority** — Channels focused on clear topics get recommended more than channels that jump between unrelated subjects.

**Current landscape:** AI-driven personalization now accounts for device, time of day, and viewing habits. Older evergreen content gets revived when topics trend again. Stronger emphasis on depth and expertise — aligned with Google's broader push toward authoritative content. Shorts and long-form have separate recommendation pipelines.

## Formats

| Format | Specs | When to use |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| Shorts | 1080x1920px (9:16), 10-35 sec ideal, up to 60 sec | Discovery and new audience — lower depth, highest reach |
| Long-form | 1920x1080px (16:9), 8-12 minutes sweet spot | Depth, monetization, subscriber growth |
| Thumbnail | 1280x720px, under 2MB, JPG/PNG | CTR is make-or-break — invest here |

**Production specs:**
- **Shorts resolution:** 1080x1920px (9:16). Clean export — no TikTok watermarks (YouTube deprioritizes them)
- **Long-form resolution:** 1920x1080px minimum. 4K if your camera supports it — YouTube rewards higher-quality uploads with better compression
- **Audio:** Clear voice is non-negotiable. Use a dedicated mic (lapel, shotgun, or USB condenser). Room echo and background noise kill watch time
- **Captions:** Upload a custom SRT file or use YouTube's auto-captions and correct errors. Captioned videos get higher completion rates and broader reach
- **End screens:** Last 20 seconds can contain clickable subscribe button and video cards. Design your close to accommodate these overlays — don't put critical content in the final 20 seconds
- **Cards:** Use in-video cards to link to related content at moments where viewers might want to go deeper. This boosts session time

## Shorts Script Template

```
SECONDS 0-2 — HOOK
"[Immediate value promise or pattern interrupt]"

Options:
- "Here's why [common belief] is wrong"
- "[Number]-second [topic] lesson"
- "The [industry] hack nobody taught me"

SECONDS 2-50 — SUBSTANCE
[Deliver value fast]
[Each point: 5-10 seconds max]
[Keep visual movement — cuts, gestures, text overlays]
[Don't stand still talking for more than 10 seconds]

SECONDS 50-60 — CLOSE
"Subscribe for more [topic]"
or "Full breakdown on my channel"
or [End abruptly — drives rewatch when viewers miss the ending]
```

## Long-Form Framework

**Hook (0-30 seconds)**
```
[Pattern interrupt or bold claim]
[Quick credibility if relevant]
[Preview the payoff]
"By the end of this video, you'll know exactly how to [outcome]"
```

**Setup (30 seconds - 1 minute)**
```
[Brief context — why this matters now]
[Who this is for]
[What you'll cover (roadmap)]
"Let's get into it"
```

**Body (main content)**
```
[Deliver on the promise]
[Clear section markers: "First..." "Second..." "Now here's the part most people miss..."]
[Proof and examples for each point]
[Every 2-3 minutes, an engagement prompt:
  "Let me know in the comments if..."
  "Hit like if this resonates"
  "Subscribe if you're getting value"]
```

**Close (final 30 seconds)**
```
[Recap the key points — short version]
[Clear next action for the viewer]
"If you want to go deeper on [related topic], watch this next"
[End screen: subscribe button + related video card]
```

## Thumbnail Design

A thumbnail has three jobs: stop the scroll, communicate the topic, create curiosity. It does this in under a second at a small size.

**Specifications:**
- **Dimensions:** 1280x720px (16:9). Always custom — never let YouTube auto-generate
- **File size:** Under 2MB. JPG or PNG
- **Elements:** 3 maximum — typically face + text + one object or background element. More than three is visual noise at thumbnail size
- **Text:** 3-5 words max. 80pt+ equivalent — must be readable at the smallest thumbnail size (the Home feed on mobile). If you can't read it when the image is 2cm wide, it's too small
- **Colors:** High contrast, saturated. Thumbnails compete against dozens of others on screen. Muted colors disappear. Yellow, red, and blue backgrounds tend to pop. Avoid colors that blend with YouTube's white/dark UI
- **Face:** If showing a face, the expression should be exaggerated — genuine surprise, intense focus, real frustration. Neutral faces don't stop scrolls. Eyes looking at camera or at the text/object creates visual direction
- **Curiosity gap:** Show the outcome, the question, or the stakes — not the process. "Before/after" thumbnails work because they create a gap the viewer needs to close
- **Consistency:** Develop a recognizable thumbnail style (same font, same layout pattern, same color palette) so returning viewers spot your videos instantly
- **No clickbait:** The thumbnail makes a promise. The video must deliver on it. Misleading thumbnails tank your satisfaction scores and hurt long-term algorithmic trust
- **Test:** YouTube Studio lets you A/B test thumbnails. Use it. Small CTR improvements compound across your entire library

## Opening Patterns

**1. Immediate Proof**
> [Show the result or transformation in the first 5 seconds, then say "here's how"]

**2. Bold Claim + Qualifier**
> "[Strong statement]. Now before you click away, let me show you exactly what I mean."

**3. Problem Identification**
> "If you've been [struggling with X], there's a reason — and it's probably not what you think."

**4. Story Opening**
> "[Short, specific anecdote]. That moment taught me something I wish I'd learned years ago."

**5. Speed Promise**
> "In the next [N] minutes, I'm going to show you [specific outcome]. No fluff, just the steps."

**6. Contrarian Position**
> "Everyone says [common advice]. I'm going to explain why that advice is costing you [result]."

**7. Curiosity Stack**
> "I tested [N] different [approaches]. Only one actually worked. Let me show you which one."

**8. Behind the Numbers**
> "[Specific impressive metric]. Most people see that number and assume [wrong conclusion]. The real story is different."

**9. Warning**
> "If you're about to [common action], stop. Watch this first. It'll save you [time/money/pain]."

**10. Direct Tutorial**
> "This is the [topic] tutorial I wish existed when I started. Zero fluff, just steps."

## What Not to Do on YouTube

- **Don't neglect the thumbnail.** Your video can be brilliant — if the thumbnail doesn't earn the click, nobody sees it. Invest in thumbnails as much as content.
- **Don't start with your intro/logo.** Nobody cares about your animated logo. Start with the hook. Earn attention before branding.
- **Don't pad for length.** The 8-12 minute sweet spot only works if the content justifies the length. A tight 6-minute video outperforms a padded 10-minute one because completion rate matters.
- **Don't ignore retention graphs.** YouTube Studio shows exactly where viewers drop off. Study this. If everyone leaves at the 2-minute mark, fix what happens at 1:50.
- **Don't upload Shorts from TikTok with the watermark.** YouTube deprioritizes content with other platform watermarks. Re-export clean.
- **Don't scatter your topics.** Topical authority matters. If your channel is about marketing, a random cooking video confuses the algorithm and your audience.
