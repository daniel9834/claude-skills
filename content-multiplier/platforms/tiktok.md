# TikTok

## How the Algorithm Works

TikTok's recommendation system tests every video with a small initial audience, then expands distribution based on engagement signals. The algorithm categorizes content by topic, emotion, and format using AI — it understands *what* your video is about, not just who engages with it.

**What matters most:**

1. **Early-second retention** — Completion rate is still the top signal, but the first 3 seconds are weighted disproportionately. If people leave in the first seconds, the video is dead.
2. **Replay rate** — Multiple watches from the same user is one of the strongest engagement signals.
3. **Shares** — Especially to DMs. Private sharing signals genuine value.
4. **Niche alignment** — The algorithm favors content that serves a specific community over content that tries to go broadly viral. Depth beats breadth.
5. **Production quality** — Good lighting, clear audio, and intentional editing are now integrated into ranking. Sloppy production gets penalized.

**Current landscape:** The algorithm increasingly favors longer content (30-60+ seconds) when retention is strong. Niche community audiences (#BookTok, #FinTok, etc.) get boosted over generic viral plays. The platform understands context — humor vs education vs emotion — and matches content to users who want that *type* of content.

## Formats

| Format | Duration | When to use |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| Quick hit | 15-30 seconds | Highest completion rate, single-point videos |
| Standard | 30-60 seconds | Now favored when retention holds — tutorials, stories |
| Deep cut | 1-3 minutes | For niche audiences who are already engaged with your content |
| Vertical | 1080x1920px (9:16) | Required — always vertical |

**Production specs:**
- **Resolution:** 1080x1920px minimum. Higher is fine, but 9:16 ratio is non-negotiable
- **Text overlays:** Bold, readable, positioned in the center-safe zone (not too close to edges where UI elements cover them). White text with dark shadow/outline works on any background. Keep to 3-8 words per overlay
- **Safe zones:** Top 15% obscured by username/description. Bottom 20% obscured by caption/buttons. Keep key visuals and text in the middle 65%
- **Audio:** Clear voice is critical. If room acoustics are bad, use a lapel mic or record in a closet. Background music should be 10-20% of voice volume, not competing with it
- **Lighting:** Natural light facing you is best. Ring lights work. Overhead fluorescents look terrible. The algorithm now factors production quality into ranking — dark, grainy video gets suppressed
- **Captions:** Always add text captions. Many viewers watch without sound. Auto-captions are fine but check them — they frequently misread words
- **Cuts and movement:** Static talking head for 30+ seconds loses viewers. Cut between angles, add b-roll, use zoom-ins on key phrases, or add visual elements every 5-8 seconds

## Script Template (15-30 seconds)

```
SECONDS 0-3 — HOOK
[Visual hook and verbal hook simultaneously]

Options:
- "Stop scrolling if you [identity marker]"
- "POV: You just realized [insight]"
- "I'm about to save you [specific time/money/pain]"
- "[Controversial statement] — let me explain"
- "Nobody talks about this part of [topic]"

SECONDS 3-25 — SUBSTANCE
"Here's what's actually going on:

[Point 1 — fast, clear, no filler]

[Point 2 — keep the momentum]

[Point 3 — the payoff, the surprising part]"

SECONDS 25-30 — CLOSE
"Follow for more [topic]"
or "Part 2?" (drives comments asking for it)
or "Save this" (drives saves)
```

## Script Template (30-60 seconds)

```
SECONDS 0-3 — HOOK
[Same hook energy as short format]

SECONDS 3-15 — SETUP
"Here's what happened / here's why this matters:
[Brief context — just enough to understand the payoff]"

SECONDS 15-50 — SUBSTANCE
[Deliver the actual value]
[Break into clear sections with verbal markers]
"First..." "Second..." "But here's the part nobody mentions..."
[Keep visual movement — don't stand still talking]

SECONDS 50-60 — CLOSE
[Strong ending — either a direct CTA or an abrupt stop that drives rewatches]
```

## Content Patterns

These are the formats that consistently perform on TikTok:

1. **Before/After** — Show the transformation immediately, then explain how
2. **Green Screen** — You in front of a tweet, article, or data screenshot
3. **Stitch/Duet** — React to trending content in your niche
4. **Day in the Life** — Niche-specific (day in the life of a [your role])
5. **POV** — "POV: You're [relatable scenario]" with insight underneath
6. **Rapid List** — "3 things that [outcome]" delivered fast with cuts
7. **Myth Bust** — "Stop believing [common misconception]" with evidence

## Opening Patterns

**1. Identity Hook**
> "This is for my [specific group] who are stuck on [specific problem]"

**2. Immediate Proof**
> [Show the result in the first frame, then explain how you got there]

**3. Controversy Spark**
> "I'm going to get pushback for this, but [claim]. Let me show you why."

**4. Speed Promise**
> "In [N] seconds I'll show you how to [specific outcome]. Ready?"

**5. Pattern Interrupt**
> [Unexpected visual or statement that breaks the scroll pattern — movement, text, or sound that doesn't match expectations]

**6. Relatable Pain**
> "If you've ever [common frustration], this is going to change everything."

**7. Tutorial in Disguise**
> "Watch me [do specific thing] in real time. This takes 30 seconds."

**8. Story Cold Open**
> "[Drop into the middle of a story with no setup]. ...and that's when I realized [insight]."

**9. Audience Split**
> "Half of you are going to agree with this. The other half are going to be furious."

**10. Future Self**
> "Your future self will thank you for learning this. Here's what nobody told me about [topic]."

## What Not to Do on TikTok

- **Don't overpolish.** TikTok rewards authenticity. A perfectly lit studio video with branded lower thirds often performs worse than someone talking naturally on their phone. The content should feel real, not produced.
- **Don't chase broad virality.** The algorithm rewards niche depth. A video that resonates deeply with 50,000 people in your niche is more valuable than one that gets 500,000 views from random users who never follow.
- **Don't front-load context.** Three seconds. That's your window. Don't start with "Hey guys, so today I wanted to talk about..." — start with the hook, then context.
- **Don't ignore audio.** Bad audio kills videos faster than bad lighting. If you're talking to camera, clear voice matters. If you're using music, make sure it's trending or at least fits the energy.
- **Don't repurpose horizontal video.** Vertical (9:16) is required. Cropped widescreen video looks lazy and gets penalized.
- **Don't force topics into video.** Some ideas are text ideas. If your source content doesn't have a natural visual or verbal hook, it's probably better as a carousel on Instagram or a thread on X.
