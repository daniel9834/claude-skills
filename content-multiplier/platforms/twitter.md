# Twitter / X

## How the Algorithm Works

The For You feed uses AI-driven ranking based on your past interactions, topic interests, and engagement patterns. The Following feed is no longer purely chronological — Grok now influences ranking there too.

**What matters most:**

1. **Replies** — Carry the most weight, especially from accounts with mutual engagement history.
2. **Quote posts** — Worth roughly double a plain retweet for engagement signals.
3. **Time on tweet** — How long someone reads your tweet or clicks through to links.
4. **Profile visits** — If your tweet makes someone tap your profile, that's a strong curiosity signal.
5. **Media presence** — Images, videos, and GIFs increase visibility scores.
6. **Velocity** — Fast early engagement in the first hours determines amplification.

**Current landscape:** The platform rewards conviction and speed. Short, dense posts with strong opinions outperform careful, balanced takes. Threads still work well for depth — the first tweet determines 90% of thread performance.

## Formats

| Format | Specs | When to use |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| Single tweet | Under 100 characters is optimal | Punchy insights, hot takes, quotable lines |
| Thread | 8-15 tweets, value in every tweet | Detailed breakdowns, stories, step-by-step |
| Quote post | Your commentary on someone else's post | 2x engagement vs plain repost |
| Image tweet | 1200x675px (16:9) | Data visuals, screenshots, quote graphics |

**Image specs:**
- **Single image:** 1200x675px (16:9) for maximum feed width. 1080x1080px (1:1) also works
- **Text on images:** Large, bold, high contrast. Readable at phone size without tapping to expand. 3-8 words max
- **Screenshots:** Crop tight — remove browser chrome, unnecessary whitespace. Highlight the relevant part with a colored border or arrow if needed
- **Quote graphics:** White or dark background, the quote in large text, attribution small at bottom. Keep it clean — no gradients, no stock photos behind text

## Thread Structure

**Tweet 1 — The hook**
```
[Bold claim or specific promise]

[One line of elaboration or proof]

A thread:
```

**Tweet 2 — Context**
```
Quick background:

[Why this matters in 2-3 lines]
[Who this is for]
```

**Tweets 3 through N — The substance**
```
[Number]. [Point in one line]

[2-3 sentences explaining or proving it]

[Example if it fits]
```

**Second-to-last — Summary**
```
The short version:

- [Point 1]
- [Point 2]
- [Point 3]
- [Point 4]
- [Point 5]
```

**Last tweet — Close**
```
If you got value from this:

1. Follow @[handle] for more
2. Repost the first tweet
3. [Specific action tied to the content]

[Link if relevant]
```

## Single Tweet Patterns

**The Insight**
```
[Counterintuitive observation]

Most people think [X].
The reality: [Y].
```

**The List**
```
[N] [things] that [specific outcome]:

- [Item]
- [Item]
- [Item]
- [Item]
- [Item]

Which one do you disagree with?
```

**The Position**
```
Unpopular take:

[Claim that challenges consensus]

Reasoning: [One sentence]
```

**The Proof**
```
[Specific result in specific timeframe]

Method:

- [Step 1]
- [Step 2]
- [Step 3]

No tricks. Just [principle].
```

**The Question**
```
[Genuinely provocative question]?

Reply with your honest answer.
```

## Opening Patterns

**1. Outcome First**
> "[Impressive result] in [timeframe]. No [common shortcut]. Here's every step:"

**2. Broken Consensus**
> "[Thing everyone agrees on] is wrong. Here's the evidence nobody wants to share."

**3. Curiosity Gap**
> "The [industry] secret that [insiders] don't talk about publicly:"

**4. Death Declaration**
> "[Popular thing] is dead. Here's what replaced it — and why most people haven't noticed."

**5. Compressed Wisdom**
> "[Number] years of [experience] compressed into [number] tweets. Save this thread."

**6. Provocative Framing**
> "This will annoy [specific group], but someone needs to say it:"

**7. Observation + Implication**
> "Nobody's talking about [specific trend]. Here's why it matters more than you think."

**8. Personal Admission**
> "I used to believe [common thing]. I was wrong. Here's what changed my mind:"

**9. Comparison Flip**
> "[Thing A] gets all the attention. [Thing B] quietly delivers better results. The data:"

**10. One-Line Bomb**
> "[Entire insight compressed into a single devastating sentence.]"

## What Not to Do on X

- **Don't write threads with filler tweets.** If a tweet doesn't add value, cut it. Short threads with density beat long threads with padding.
- **Don't hedge.** "I think maybe possibly this could work" gets scrolled past. Take a position.
- **Don't ask for engagement without earning it.** "Like and RT!" at the end of a mediocre thread doesn't work. Earn the repost.
- **Don't cross-post LinkedIn content.** The cultures are different. LinkedIn's polished, structured tone reads as try-hard on X.
- **Don't ignore replies.** Engaging in your replies within the first hour amplifies the original tweet.
- **Don't use hashtags.** They look spammy on X. The algorithm doesn't need them — it reads your content.
